Post Election Model Reflection

After a long semester of model making and an even longer campaign season, the midterm elections are finally in the rearview. To the surprise of many, no red wave came despite an increasingly dire media narrative and tightening polls in the days leading up to the midterm elections. While final vote tallies are still being counted we have a good enough idea of where the final tally stands. As of November 22nd, Democrats have held the Senate and await a Runoff election in Georgia to determine if they will expand their slim majority.

Final Midterm Election Model

Blog 7

This week we looked at shocks and unexpected events in elections. Shocks are inherently difficult to operationalize and incorporate into models because their nature is that they produce one off events with no clear data to train a model on. The literature looking at shocks has produced an inconclusive lack of consensus. Some studies show even large shocks don’t produce lasting impacts on voter opinion. Others show that even shocks unrelated to politics such as Shark attacks or natural disasters can impact voter behavior (Achen and Bartels).

Blog 6: Turnout

This week in election analytics we examined the impact of voter turnout on election outcomes. Historically, increases in voter turnout have been thought to benefit Democrats. However, in the post-Trump Republican party, this relationship has been called into question. In the Post-Trump era, Republicans have tapped into a new base, largely non-college educated white males, who are non-reliable voters (unlike their traditional base of wealthy whites and the elderly, who vote reliably).

Blog Post 2: Economic Indicators

Electoral Predictions and forecasts often cite the “political environment” that a given election cycle is taking place in. For example, pundits could cite a growing economy as cause for a good environment for incumbents while troubling foreign policy incidents might add to a bad environment for incumbents. These “fundamentals” exist outside the campaign’s control and set the stage for a given election cycle. But, how accurately can we predict elections with these conditions alone?
